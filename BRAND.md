# Distribution Labs: Brand Identity Mandate

## 1. Core Philosophy: "The Expert Narrator"
The visual identity must bridge the gap between high-level storytelling (Expertise) and technical execution (AI). It avoids "generic tech" gradients in favor of high-contrast, editorial-style layouts.

## 2. Color Math (Source: Lab Palette)
We use a high-contrast foundation with "Strategic Accents" to signal action and innovation.
- **Base (Authority):** `##191514` (Warm Black). Use for deep backgrounds.
- **Surface (Clarity):** `#edece3` (Off White). Use for primary text and clean containers.
- **Action (Energy):** `##ff5402` (Orange). Use sparingly for high-impact CTAs and badges.
- **AI Accent (Modernity):** `##a2a1f8` (Sky). Use for subtle glows and data indicators.
- **Muted (Subtext):** `##a38c97` (Gray). Use for sub-headers and secondary information.
- **Other** '#f0a7c6' (pink). Use with discretion or when asked for more options.
-**Other2** '#505aff' (blue). Use with discretion or when asked for more options.
-**Other3** '#98991f' (olive). Use with discretion or when asked for more options.
-**Other4** '#096331' (Green). Use with discretion or when asked for more options.
-**Other5** '#73331d' (Brown). Use with discretion or when asked for more options.

## 3. Typographic Hierarchy: "The Scale of Wisdom"
We use a high-contrast pairing to distinguish between "The Story" and "The Logic."
- **Headlines (The Story):** `Instrument Serif` (Primary), `Optima` (Secondary).
  - *Logic:* Large scale, wide tracking (`0.05em`), and light weights to feel "Prestige."
- **Body & UI (The Logic):** `Instrument Sans` (Primary), `Helvetica Now Condensed` (Secondary).
  - *Logic:* Geometric, tight leading, and heavy weights for navigation to feel "Precise."
- **Contrast Rule:** Hero Headlines must be at least **3.5x** the size of body text.

## 4. Component Geometry: "Softened Precision"
Founders want "Smart" (precise) but "Partners" (approachable).
- **Border-Radii:** `12px`. Avoid sharp `0px` (too cold) and bubbly `100px` (too consumer).
- **Whitespace:** "The 60/40 Rule." At least 60% of any section should be whitespace to maintain an "uncluttered" and "premium" feel without feeling empty.
- **Shadows:** No hard borders. Use "The Deep Float"—soft, multi-layered shadows to create depth.

## 5. Tone & Voice
- **Direct:** Avoid bureaucratic jargon.
- **Punchy:** Use short sentences and strong verbs.
- **Expert-Peer:** Speak as a partner who has been in the trenches, not an outside analyst.
